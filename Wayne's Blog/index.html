Pre-war shophouses

The Shophouse, a reminder of architecture past, is a common sight in neighbourhoods such as Joo Chiat, Emerald Hill and Tanjong Pagar. Singapore has more than 6,500 conserved shophouses, which were built between the early 1800s and mid 1900s.

Architecture and Facade

You can tell which era these classy beauties were designed and built in just by looking at their facade and detailing. For example, the first shophouses here were short with one or two timber windows on the second floor and little decoration on the outside. Later in the 1930s, Art Deco-styled shophouses, which used Shanghai plaster and had column orders and arches, came into fashion. Today, many young home owners are giving theses shophouses new leases of life, remodelling the interiors and restoring the faded facades to their former glory.

Conservation Districts

The Historic Districts of Boat Quay, Chinatown, Kampong Glam and Little India. The Residential Historic Districts of Emerald Hill, Cairnhill and Blair Plain. The Good Class Bungalow Areas and the Mountbatten Road Conservation Area.
